# Scalable Fingerprinting Distinction

Scalable fingerprinting scales implanted fingerprint identities. In
`natural_evidence_v1`, the opportunity bank scales context-conditioned,
next-token-measurable opportunities in ordinary outputs.

A bank entry is not a fingerprint. It is a cached sample of a bucket-construction
policy at one prefix:

```text
prefix h + audit key K + tokenizer/reference distribution + policy -> buckets B_K(h, b)
```

The entry becomes evidence only after a payload is encoded into bucket ids, a
protected model is trained to raise target bucket mass at eligible natural
prefixes, transcripts are committed, and the public verifier reconstructs
observations from generated text after key reveal.

Therefore the 24,576-entry target may be described only as opportunity-bank
scale. It must not be written as 24,576 inserted fingerprints or as ownership
recovery evidence by itself.

The paper-facing comparison should report:

- opportunity coverage,
- effective bits per response,
- payload recovery after training,
- raw-model, wrong-key, and wrong-payload false accepts,
- naturalness and utility cost,
- sanitizer cost relative to a visible structured carrier baseline.

Do not compare raw counts of Scalable fingerprints and opportunity-bank entries
as if they are the same object.
